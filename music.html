Traditional Dance Performance

Traditional Dances & Instruments

  • Mokhibo - Performed by women, involving shoulder movements.
  • Mohobelo - Men's traditional stomping dance.
  • Setapo - Slow-paced dance by elderly women.
  • Liphotha - War dance symbolizing strength.
  • Moraba - A hunting rhythm dance.
  • Lesiba - A mouth-resonated musical bow instrument.
  • Thomo - Traditional harp used in storytelling.
  • Likhitlane - Rattle used in dance.
  • Drum - Beaten to direct pace of dance.
  • Leqoqo - Wooden clapper for rhythm.
  • Sekhankula - String instrument made from tin & string.
  • Dinaka - Reed flutes for melodic sound.
  • Lere - Bell-like metal instrument.
  • Lira - Used in ritual music.
  • Clapping - Used to support dancers rhythmically.
  • Chanting - Traditional chants during dances.
  • Whistling - Accompanies fast-paced dances.
  • Gumboots - Worn in modern traditional fusion dances.
  • Solo Performances - Poetic storytelling in dance.
  • Group Circle Dances - Celebration dances.
  • Initiation Dances - Symbolizing transition into adulthood.
  • Wedding Dances - Celebratory traditional moves.
  • Harvest Festival Dances - Performed after a good season.
